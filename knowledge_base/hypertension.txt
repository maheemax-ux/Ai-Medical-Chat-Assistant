Hypertension (High Blood Pressure) Overview

Hypertension is a condition in which the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high, typically defined as a reading at or above 130/80 mmHg (definitions vary slightly by guideline).

Hypertension is often called a "silent" condition because it frequently has no obvious symptoms until it causes complications. Some people may experience headaches, shortness of breath, or nosebleeds, but these are not reliable indicators.

Risk factors include family history, high salt intake, obesity, physical inactivity, excessive alcohol consumption, smoking, and chronic stress.

Management includes lifestyle modification (reduced sodium intake, regular exercise, weight management, limiting alcohol) and medications such as ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers, or diuretics, prescribed based on individual patient factors.

Uncontrolled hypertension significantly increases the risk of heart attack, stroke, kidney disease, and heart failure. Regular blood pressure monitoring is recommended, especially for those with risk factors.
