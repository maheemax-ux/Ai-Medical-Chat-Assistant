Common Cold vs Influenza (Flu)

The common cold and influenza are both respiratory illnesses caused by different viruses, and their symptoms can overlap, making them sometimes hard to distinguish without testing.

Common cold symptoms usually develop gradually and include a runny or stuffy nose, sneezing, sore throat, mild cough, and slight fatigue. Fever is uncommon in adults with a cold.

Influenza symptoms tend to appear more suddenly and severely, including high fever, chills, body aches, significant fatigue, dry cough, and headache. Flu can lead to complications such as pneumonia, particularly in young children, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems.

General care for both includes rest, adequate fluid intake, and over-the-counter symptom relief (e.g., antipyretics for fever, decongestants). Antiviral medications may be prescribed for flu in certain high-risk patients if started early.

Seek medical attention if experiencing difficulty breathing, persistent chest pain, confusion, severe or persistent vomiting, or symptoms that improve then return with fever and worse cough.
